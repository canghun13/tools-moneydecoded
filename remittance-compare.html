Common Questions
International Wire Transfer FAQ

Is Wise actually safe and regulated? ▾
Yes. Wise is regulated by the FCA (UK), FinCEN (US), and equivalent bodies in every country it operates. Customer money is held in segregated accounts at tier-1 banks — separate from Wise's own funds. They've processed over $100 billion in transfers since 2011.
Why do banks charge so much more? ▾
Two layers: a flat wire fee ($30–$50) plus an FX markup of 2–4% buried in the exchange rate. The second part is invisible — your bank quotes you a rate silently worse than the real market rate and keeps the difference. On a $10,000 transfer, that markup alone can cost $200–$400.
When is Remitly better than Wise? ▾
Remitly occasionally runs promotional rates that undercut Wise on specific corridors (e.g., USD→INR or USD→PHP) and is faster for cash pickup. But for most USD→EUR/GBP/AUD transfers, Wise's rate is more consistent outside of promotions. Always compare in real-time before sending.
Are there transfer limits with Wise? ▾
Wise allows personal transfers up to $1 million+ per transaction on most corridors after identity verification. Limits vary by destination. The ID check takes about 5–10 minutes in the app.
Does the recipient need a Wise account? ▾
No. You send to a regular bank account number — your recipient never needs to sign up for anything. Wise converts the currency and deposits local currency directly into their bank account.
What is the "mid-market rate" and why does it matter? ▾
The mid-market rate is the midpoint between buy and sell currency prices — what you see on Google or XE.com. Banks give you a rate worse than this and pocket the spread. Wise uses the true mid-market rate and charges a small transparent fee on top, so you always know exactly what you're paying.
